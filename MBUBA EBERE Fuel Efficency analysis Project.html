The most strongly correlated and weakly correlated pair of the variables in the dataset¶

In [11]:
# Calculating the correlation matrix
correlation_matrix = FuelEfficiency.corr()

# using heatmap to show the correlation matrix
plt.figure(figsize=(8, 7))
sns.heatmap(correlation_matrix, annot=True, cmap='coolwarm', fmt=".2f")
plt.title("The Heatmap Of The Correlation Matrix")
plt.show()

# Finding the most strongly and most weakly correlated pairs
strongest_pair = None
weakest_pair = None
max_correlation = 0
min_correlation = 1

for i in range(len(correlation_matrix.columns)):
    for j in range(i+1, len(correlation_matrix.columns)):
        # Skipping the diagonal elements and identical pairs
        if i != j:
            correlation = abs(correlation_matrix.iloc[i, j])
            if correlation > max_correlation:
                max_correlation = correlation
                strongest_pair = (correlation_matrix.columns[i], correlation_matrix.columns[j])
            if 0 < correlation < min_correlation:
                min_correlation = correlation
                weakest_pair = (correlation_matrix.columns[i], correlation_matrix.columns[j])

# Printing the results
print("Most strongly correlated variables is:", strongest_pair, "with the Correlation of:", max_correlation)
print("Most weakly correlated variables is:", weakest_pair, "with the Correlation of:", min_correlation)

Determining the correlation between weight in pounds and litres of fuel consumed per 100 km on the highway for cars < 4000 pounds¶

In [15]:
# Calculating correlation for all cars
correlation_all_cars = FuelEfficiency['Weight_in_Pounds'].corr(FuelEfficiency['Highway_L/100km'])

# Filtering cars with weight less than 4000 pounds
filtered_data = FuelEfficiency[FuelEfficiency['Weight_in_Pounds'] < 4000]

# Calculating correlation for cars with weight less than 4000 pounds
correlation_filtered = filtered_data['Weight_in_Pounds'].corr(filtered_data['Highway_L/100km'])


# Visualize the correlation for cars with weight less than 4000 pounds
plt.figure(figsize=(8, 6))
sns.scatterplot(x=filtered_data['Weight_in_Pounds'], y=filtered_data['Highway_L/100km'])
plt.title("Correlation for Cars with Weight < 4000 Pounds And Highway Fuel Consumption")
plt.xlabel("Weight (in Pounds)")
plt.ylabel("Highway Fuel Consumption (L/100km)")
plt.show()

# Print the correlation values
print("Correlation for all cars is:", correlation_all_cars)
print("Correlation for cars with weight < 4000 pounds is:", correlation_filtered)

The correlation for all cars (0.9062) indicates a strong positive correlation between weight and highway fuel consumption, suggesting that as the weight of the cars increases, their fuel consumption on the highway tends to also increase.

The correlation for cars with weight less than 4000 pounds (0.7243) is slightly lower than the correlation for all cars. This suggests that within the subset of lighter cars, the relationship between weight and highway fuel consumption is still positive but not as strong as in the entire dataset.
